About WIC

Community

In the traditionally male-dominated field of computer science, having a supportive community can be a game-changer. Our club at the University of Kansas is dedicated to creating a welcoming space where women and non-binary individuals in tech can connect, collaborate, and thrive. By joining us, you’ll find a network of close friends and mentors who understand the unique challenges you face and are here to offer support and guidance. Participate in enriching discussions, skill-building workshops, and collaborative projects designed to enhance your professional and personal growth. Together, we’re building a strong community that empowers each member to excel in the tech world.

Industry

Connect with leading tech companies to provide industry insights and resources that can help with your learning experience Organize workshops and seminars led by industry professionals, offering real-world advice regarding internships and work experience Seeking out corporate sponsorships to support club events and activities, ensuring sustained engagement and providing companies with a platform to showcase their commitment to diversity and inclusion in technology. Provides training to help women become proficient in essential tech skills. Once skills are acquired, KUWIC connects participants with externships at various companies for real-world experience.

Grace Hopper

The Grace Hopper Celebration is the world’s largest gathering of women and non-binary technologists. Offers unparalleled networking opportunities with peers and industry leaders. Attend inspiring keynote speeches, panel discussions, and technical sessions. Participate in workshops designed to enhance professional development. Gain access to internships at top companies like Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, Uber, Los Alamos, CIA/FBI, and USAA.

Girl Code

Facilitate engaging programming workshops for middle school girls, introducing them to coding fundamentals and starting their passion for technology. Organize interactive sessions that provide practical experience with coding tools and techniques, helping participants develop a solid foundation in programming and problem-solving. Manage outreach initiatives and coordinated events, ensuring the smooth execution of workshops and activities.
